2. Information We Collect and Why

The use of our products and services may require user information input and consent for certain activities such as in-app purchases (if applicable), technical support, or participation in specific features.

We collect information to provide and improve our products, offer support, process transactions, and personalize user experience. This information may include:

Information You Provide:

  • Contact Information (e.g., name, email address when contacting support or creating an optional account).
  • Parent's email address, password, child's name/display name, age range, and parent's mobile number (Only if our product includes features specifically requiring parental involvement and account creation, subject to parental consent requirements like COPPA).
  • Information provided during support requests (e.g., name, device information, description of the issue).

Information Collected Automatically:

  • Device Information: Brand and model of your mobile device, operating system version, device identifiers (such as Advertising ID - IDFA for iOS, AAID for Android), language settings, IP address.
  • Usage Information: How you interact with our products (e.g., features used, levels played, session duration, achievements unlocked, in-app events). This is often collected via analytics tools.
  • Transaction Information: If you make in-app purchases, transaction details are processed by the relevant app store (Apple App Store, Google Play Store), and while we don't typically receive your full credit card number, we may receive transaction confirmation data.
  • Location Information: We may collect coarse location (e.g., country or region based on IP address) for analytics or content customization. If we need precise location data for a specific game feature (e.g., location-based gameplay), we will ask for your explicit permission first. You can typically disable location services at the device level or within the application's settings.
  • Internet Connection: Our products need to access the internet to connect to servers for core functionality, download updates, validate licenses (if applicable), and provide online features. (Data fees may be charged by your carrier).

Specific Examples of Data Collection Scenarios:

2.1 When using product features requiring server interaction (e.g., leaderboards, cloud saves).
2.2 When requesting technical support.
2.3 When our products connect to servers for updates or core online functionality.
2.4 To provide analytics on product usage to understand user behavior and improve our products. We use device identifiers (like Advertising ID) and usage information for this purpose.
2.5 If a feature requires access to your contacts (e.g., to find friends who also use our products), we will request your explicit permission before accessing them.

Child Users:

2.6 We are mindful of collecting information from children. If a product is directed towards children under 13 (or applicable age), we limit personal information collection to persistent identifiers for internal operations (like analytics and ensuring functionality) unless we obtain verifiable parental consent.
2.7 A child's participation or access to an activity cannot be conditioned on him or her providing more Personal Information than is reasonably necessary for that activity.

8. Tracking Technologies (SDKs, Advertising IDs)

We and our third-party partners use various tracking technologies within our mobile applications:

8.1 SDKs (Software Development Kits): Our partners (like analytics providers) provide SDKs that are integrated into our apps. These SDKs allow them (and us) to collect device and usage data for analytics, performance monitoring, and potentially ad serving.

8.2 Advertising Identifiers: We may use your device's Advertising Identifier (IDFA on iOS, AAID on Android) for analytics and, if applicable, personalized advertising. You can typically reset your Advertising ID or limit ad tracking through your device's settings.

8.3 Analytics Software: We use mobile analytics software (potentially from third parties listed in Section 15) to better understand the functionality and usage of our products. This software may record information such as app usage frequency, events within the app, aggregated usage, performance data, and download source. We generally do not link analytics data to personally identifiable information submitted elsewhere in the app, but anonymous or pseudonymized data is used.

8.4 Pixels/Beacons: We may use tracking pixels in promotional emails (if you opt-in to receive them) to determine if emails have been opened and acted upon, helping us tailor future communications.
